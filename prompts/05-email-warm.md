# Email — Warm sequence

Write the first outreach email for a WARM lead (ICP score 4–7.9). Problem-led nurture.

## Constraints

- **Length**: 100–140 words. Subject ≤ 10 words.
- **Frame**: lead with a problem the recipient probably feels (slow shipping cycles, fragmented stack, generic data tooling). One concrete reference to their context.
- **Tone**: helpful, not desperate. You're offering a perspective, not asking for time.
- **CTA**: a two-option close — "happy to share a 3-minute Loom, or just reply with X" — lower commitment than the hot email.
- **Signoff**: `Teodor`.
- **Banned phrases**: same as hot.

## Iteration notes

- **v1**: warm emails sounded identical to hot ones, just longer. Forced a "lead with the problem" pattern so warm reads like a teaching email and hot reads like a peer email.
- **v2**: original CTA was the same single-ask as hot. Reframed to a two-option close — gives a lower-friction path while still asking for the meeting.
- **What I'd iterate next**: try a single-paragraph variant (one continuous thought) vs the current 2-paragraph structure to see which gets higher reply rates.
